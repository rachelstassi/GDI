Bystander Intervention

Everyone can take an active role in preventing sexual violence, stalking and dating/partner violence by making the choice to intervene. If you see a situation that might lead to sexual violence, hear a sexist comment or joke, or are worried for someone’s safety: speak up, it’s our place! Your actions could stop violent behavior before it starts. Bystander intervention does not have to be confrontational. There is no one way to be an active bystander. Intervene in a way that feels right to you and in a way that will keep you safe.

Illuminate is the prevention program on campus and the Illuminate website provides more information about bystander intervention trainings.

Some of the options you have to intervene include direct, delegate, or distract:

  • Direct – you are directly interacting with the potential perpetrator or victim and addressing the concern.
  • Delegate – asking or telling someone else what to do to help address the concern (i.e. friend, bartender, other bystanders, police).
  • Distract – this is the art of diversion: you attempt to pull the attention of the people involved away from the situation.
