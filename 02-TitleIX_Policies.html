Title IX of the Educational Amendments Act

Title IX

 

is a federal law that states “no person in the United States shall, on the basis of sex, be excluded from participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination under any education program or activity receiving Federal financial assistance.”

In other words, Title IX says all students have an equal right to education at PSU regardless of their gender or sex. Specifically addressing issues that arise from gender/sex discrimination, stalking, sexual harassment, sexual misconduct (including sexual exploitation and non-consensual sexual contact) and sexual assault.

PSU Policies You Need to Know About:

In addition to the federal Title IX law, PSU has internal policies that address your safety and they are:

PSU Student Code of Conduct

The Student Conduct Code describes the rights and responsibilities of all PSU students and student organizations. The Student Conduct Code addresses issues of sexual misconduct (including sexual exploitation, non-consensual sexual contact, and non consensual sexual intercourse), sexual harassment, stalking and dating or domestic violence.

The Office of the Dean of Student Life administers the Student Code of Conduct.

PSU's Prohibited Discrimination and Harassment Policy

The Prohibited Discrimination and Harassment Policy addresses issues of sex/gender discrimination, sexual harassment, sexual assault, and discrimination/harassment based on any protected class. Other protected classes include, but are not limited to, age, disability, national origin, race, color, marital status, veteran status, religion, sexual orientation, gender identity and expression, genetic information, or any other basis protected by federal, state or local law.

If you experience sexual assault or harassment at any time, you can get help from a confidential Advocate at the Women’s Resource Center, Queer Resource Center or Pan African Commons. Advocates help students of all genders identify which services will work best for them. To be connected with a confidential advocate, please call 503-725-5672 or schedule an appointment here.

In instances of non-confidential reporting, the Office of the Dean of Student Life investigates complaints against students, and Global Diversity & Inclusion investigates complaints against faculty and all other employees. We will go over exactly how to make a report later in this training.
